# Exemplar — concept-map mode

Index: representation rule → property that falls out → design questions.

---

## Representation

Treat an organization as a graph: **nodes = departments**, **edges = shared
responsibility**. A complete graph is maximal entanglement—everyone shares a
direct edge with everyone else.

## What falls out

Degree becomes a decentralization proxy: high degree means many shared duties;
isolated nodes are single points of process failure. Paths matter too—if sales
reaches treasury only via marketing **or** operations, that is a two-edge
dependency whether or not anyone drew an org chart that way.

## Questions the map raises

Should sales–treasury be a direct edge, or is the two-hop path intentional
control? Graph language does not answer the policy question; it makes the
policy **visible**. That visibility is the insight.
