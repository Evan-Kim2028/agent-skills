# Exemplar — systems-essay mode

Index: landscape → mechanism A/B → costed pick. Domain-agnostic shape.

---

## 1.0 The growth constraint

The industry’s bottleneck is not marketing. It is a design limit: systems that
scale issuance without a credible stabilization rule eventually tax their own
users—or outsource trust to a custodian.

## 2.0 Design A — dual-token seigniorage

When demand rises, the system expands supply against a floating pair asset. When
demand falls, contraction depends on buyers of the volatile leg. Growth policy is
explicit; the failure mode is reflexive unwind if the pair asset cannot absorb
exits.

## 3.0 Design B — credit-based stabilization

Peg defense leans on internal credit and time-weighted claims rather than a
second free-floating mint/burn partner. Demand is pulled into the mechanism via
yield on deposits, not only via speculative pair dynamics.

## 4.0 Pick

If the goal is **credible expansion under stress**, credit-based rules beat pure
seigniorage theater—because they name the debt and the incentive to hold through
contraction. If the goal is **maximum reflexive growth**, dual-token wins until
it doesn’t. The tradeoff is the point; “decentralized” alone is not a mechanism.
