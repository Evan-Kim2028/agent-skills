# Exemplar — forum-fragment mode

Index: partial observation; open questions as first-class ending.

---

Until recently the market sat near the floor fee, with brief spikes when demand
clustered. That calm is not a law of nature—it is a low-competition regime.

When competition shows up, fee and inclusion dynamics stop looking like a flat
line. Margins for participants who buy the resource look less like a monopoly
utility and more like a contested auction—exact numbers depend on the window and
the cohort you measure.

## Open questions

* What do margins look like for newer entrants once the floor is no longer free?
* Do priority tips and base fees diverge under stress, or move together?
* Would a higher minimum fee damp spikes, or only shift competition into another
  channel?

These are the point of the note. A soft “in conclusion, competition matters”
would erase them.
