## Brief

The opening lesson of a free online course — the "why this course, why now, what you'll build"
piece before any code. Global audience (the course is in English, students from everywhere; the
facts contain no community or regional angle — the piece should carry zero geography). Motivate
without hype-washing; be honest about the effort required.

## Fact-sheet (FROZEN — course structure is fixed for the test; do not invent extra modules)

- Course: "Solana Development Fundamentals" — free, online, self-paced with weekly checkpoints.
- 6 modules over 8 weeks:
  1. Solana core concepts (accounts, transactions, programs)
  2. Rust for Solana (only the Rust you need)
  3. Anchor framework (program development)
  4. Client development (TypeScript, wallet adapter)
  5. Testing and security (LiteSVM, common vulnerability classes)
  6. Capstone: ship a full dApp (program + frontend) to devnet
- Prerequisites: comfort in ANY programming language. No Rust, no blockchain background required.
- Time commitment: 5–7 hours/week. Honest expectation: weeks 2–3 (Rust) are the hardest stretch;
  most dropouts happen there.
- Outcome: by week 8, students have deployed a working program to devnet and built a frontend that
  talks to it — a portfolio piece, not a certificate.
- Tooling is free: devnet SOL comes from faucets; no money needed to complete the course.
