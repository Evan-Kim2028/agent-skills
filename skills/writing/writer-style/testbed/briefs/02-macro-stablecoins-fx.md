## Brief

A macro piece: why stablecoin adoption is strongest where local currencies are weakest, what that
means for FX access in emerging markets, and why the domestic-payments story (PIX) and the
dollar-access story are different problems. Make the economics legible; numbers as proof.

Note for the harness: this brief's TOPIC is the region — LATAM/Brazil lexemes here are substance,
not identity beats, so there is no mechanical count assertion. Human-rated cell: present but not
saturated, opportunity framing, no grievance.

## Fact-sheet (FROZEN — compiled 2026-07-02; macro figures are approximations from 2024–2025 public
reporting and must be styled as approximations, not precision claims)

- Stablecoins are tokenized dollars; supply is dominated by USDT and USDC (combined supply on the
  order of $250B+ as of 2025 reporting).
- The emerging-market use case is dollar access without a US bank account: a savings hedge against
  local-currency depreciation and a cheap cross-border settlement rail.
- Brazil: the central bank (BCB) has publicly stated that roughly 90% of Brazilian crypto flows are
  stablecoins (Campos Neto, 2024 statements). The real traded roughly between 4.8 and 6.2 BRL/USD
  across 2020–2025.
- Brazil already solved DOMESTIC instant payments with PIX (central-bank rail, launched Nov 2020,
  free and instant). Stablecoins are not competing with PIX — they matter for the dollar
  store-of-value and cross-border legs that PIX does not cover.
- Argentina: the peso lost more than 90% of its dollar value across 2019–2024; USDT is widely used
  as a savings vehicle.
- Remittances: Latin America + Caribbean inflows are on the order of $150B/year (World Bank
  2023–2024 era reporting); the global average cost of a traditional remittance corridor is ~6%.
- A stablecoin transfer on Solana settles in seconds and costs a fraction of a cent (base fee 5,000
  lamports per signature).
- FX-hedged, dollar-denominated yield accessible from a phone is a product traditional local
  banking in these markets does not offer retail.
